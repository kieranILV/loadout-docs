# Backing Projects

Backing a project on Loadout means buying the token. Every purchase contributes trading fees to the project's treasury. You're not just speculating — you're funding.

## How to Back a Project

1. **Find a project** — Browse trending, new launches, or search for games you like
2. **Evaluate it** — Check the demo, docs, team, roadmap, treasury progress
3. **Connect wallet** — Solana wallet (Phantom, Solflare, etc.) or sign in with passkey
4. **Buy the token** — Enter amount, confirm transaction
5. **Done** — You now hold the project's token

That's it. No whitelist. No waiting period. No complicated vesting. Just buy.

## What You're Actually Buying

When you buy a project token on Loadout:

- **You get tokens immediately** — No lockups, no cliffs
- **You can sell anytime** — Liquidity is always available
- **4.75% of your trade goes to the game** — Treasury funding
- **You're betting on the game's success** — If it ships and does well, token value should reflect that

It's speculation with purpose. Your trading activity directly funds the thing you're speculating on.

## Evaluating Projects

Before you ape, check the basics:

### Must-Haves
- **Playable demo** — Can you actually play something?
- **Team info** — Who's building this? Any track record?
- **Design documentation** — Do they know what they're building?
- **Roadmap** — What's the plan?

### Good Signs
- Active updates and communication
- Growing holder count
- Treasury being used (not just sitting)
- Community forming (Discord, socials)
- Demo improvements over time

### Red Flags
- No demo, just "coming soon"
- Anonymous team with no history
- Vague roadmap
- Treasury draining with no visible progress
- Dead socials

## The 5% Fee

Yes, there's a 5% fee on every trade. That's the point.

Think of it like NFT royalties — when NFTs had 5% creator fees, traders accepted it because "the artist gets paid." Same logic here. The game gets funded.

If you're day-trading purely for quick flips, Loadout probably isn't your platform. If you're backing games you believe in with a longer time horizon, the fee is what makes the whole model work.

## Before vs After Graduation

### Pre-Graduation
- Trading happens on bonding curve
- 5% fee (4.75% treasury, 0.25% protocol)
- Project working toward 150 SOL treasury threshold

### Post-Graduation
- Trading moves to Meteora DLMM pool
- Same 5% fee structure
- Deeper liquidity, tighter spreads
- Project has proven initial demand

Graduation doesn't change what you hold — your tokens are the same. It just means the project hit a milestone and now trades on better infrastructure.

## Risk

Let's be clear: most projects will fail.

Games are hard to ship. Teams overestimate what they can deliver. Hype fades. Attention moves elsewhere. This is true for all of game development, and Loadout doesn't change that.

What Loadout does:
- Makes it easier to evaluate projects early
- Provides liquidity so you can exit if things go wrong
- Creates transparency around treasury and progress

What Loadout doesn't do:
- Guarantee any project will succeed
- Protect you from bad investments
- Promise returns

Size your positions accordingly. Don't bet what you can't lose.
