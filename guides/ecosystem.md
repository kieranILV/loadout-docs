# The Ecosystem

Loadout creates a flywheel where traders, studios, and the platform all benefit from the same activity: trading.

<figure><img src="../diagrams/ecosystem-flow.svg" alt="Loadout Ecosystem"><figcaption>The Loadout flywheel</figcaption></figure>

## The Core Loop

It's simple:

1. **Good game gets attention** → People want to back it
2. **Traders buy in** → Price moves, volume happens
3. **Trading fees accumulate** → 4.75% of every trade goes to treasury
4. **Studio gets funded** → Real money to build with
5. **Studio ships updates** → Game gets better
6. **Token value reflects progress** → Market reprices
7. **More attention** → Loop repeats

When it works, it compounds. The best projects create virtuous cycles where attention feeds funding feeds progress feeds more attention.

## Key Participants

### Studios

Game developers who launch projects on Loadout. They bring:
- Playable demos
- Design documentation
- Team credentials
- Development roadmap

In return, they get:
- Funding without publishers or equity dilution
- Capital that scales with demand
- Built-in community of backers
- Distribution surface for their game

### Backers

Traders who buy and sell project tokens. They get:
- Early access to promising games
- Fair entry — same terms as everyone
- Liquidity from day one
- Potential upside if the game succeeds

Their activity provides:
- Price discovery
- Treasury funding via fees
- Market signal for project quality

### Loadout Protocol

The platform that connects studios and backers. We provide:
- Launch infrastructure
- Trading mechanics (bonding curve → Meteora DLMM)
- Discovery and curation
- Graduation automation

Funded by 0.25% of trading fees.

## Value Flows

Every trade moves value through the system:

| Flow | What Happens |
|------|--------------|
| Trader → Token | Buying creates demand, price rises |
| Trade → Treasury | 4.75% of every swap funds development |
| Treasury → Studio | Real SOL to pay team, contractors, marketing |
| Studio → Game | Playable content ships |
| Game → Token | Success increases token value |
| Token → Trader | Holders benefit from game progress |

The fee is what makes it work. It's not friction — it's the funding mechanism. Think of it like NFT royalties. Nobody complained about 5% going to artists. Same energy here.

## Market-Driven Curation

Loadout doesn't pick winners. The market does.

Projects that capture attention and trading activity accumulate treasury and graduate. Projects that don't, fade. Natural selection through capital flows.

Over time, this creates quality sorting without manual gatekeeping:
- **Good projects** → Volume → Treasury → Graduate → Ship → More volume
- **Bad projects** → No volume → No treasury → Fade out

The cream rises. The noise settles. The platform doesn't need to decide what's good — it just needs to make the rules consistent.

## Why This Works

The model works because incentives align:

- **Traders want games to succeed** — their bags depend on it
- **Studios want trading volume** — that's how they get funded
- **Loadout wants both** — platform grows when projects thrive

Everyone's pulling in the same direction. That's rare in crypto gaming, and it's the whole point.
